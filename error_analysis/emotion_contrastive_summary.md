# Emotion Contrastive Error Analysis: Executive Summary Table

Here is a synthesized summary table of the algorithmic blindspots and actionable dataset strategies:

| Core Algorithmic Blindspot | Description | Actionable Dataset Strategy |
| :------------------------- | :---------- | :-------------------------- |
| **1. Misinterpreting Cumulative Impact & Socio-economic Factors** | The model struggles to infer emotion (Low, Medium, High) from the *cumulative weight* of repeated issues, severe financial strain, disruption to essential services, or threats to basic needs, often requiring explicit, high-impact keywords. | Augment training data with examples where emotion is conveyed through a detailed history of unresolved issues, severe socio-economic/safety impacts, or persistent frustration, even without explicit "trigger" words or legalistic threats. |
| **2. Over-reliance on Overt Emotional/Aggressive Language** | The model over-indexes on explicit personal frustration, exhaustion, or aggressive "power phrases" (e.g., legal threats, refusal), misclassifying intensity when the tone is formal, procedural, or the emotion is implied rather than explicitly stated. | Add examples where: 'Medium' includes personal frustration but maintains a formal tone with specific demands; 'High' is conveyed through formal, non-negotiable demands and detailed grievances without explicit legal/aggressive phrasing; 'Low' includes mild frustration/rhetorical questions but remains calm. |
| **3. Under-recognition of Urgency in Early Stages or Internal Escalation** | The model struggles to identify 'Medium' or 'High' urgency/severity in initial contacts due to critical system failures, or when the escalation is internal (repeated failed attempts) rather than involving explicit external threats (e.g., ombudsman, regulatory bodies). | Include more training examples where: 'High' emotion is driven by extreme frustration and detailed *internal* failed resolution attempts; 'Medium' urgency is present in initial contacts due to critical system failures or significant administrative oversights. |
| **4. Calibrating Intensity for Specific Demands & Consequences (Low vs. Medium)** | The model misinterprets the presence of specific negative impacts or direct, time-bound demands as inherently 'Medium' intensity, failing to recognize that these can exist within a 'Low' emotional state if the overall tone lacks aggression or heightened agitation. | Add more training examples where 'Low' emotion includes significant negative impact and direct, time-bound demands but maintains an overall calm, non-aggressive tone, especially when explicit mild emotion words are absent. |
| **5. Under-prioritization of Critical Safety Risks & Legal Duties of Care** | The model under-weights the severity and urgency conveyed by formal descriptions of immediate, critical safety risks, legal duties of care, or the vulnerability of individuals, especially when explicit financial threats or direct emotional outbursts are not the primary drivers. | Augment data with examples where 'High' emotion/urgency is conveyed through formal descriptions of critical safety risks, legal obligations, or the impact on vulnerable individuals, even without explicit financial penalties or overt emotional language. |
